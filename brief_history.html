Ilya Minin (Eli)

Artist Statement

My practice interrogates the fragmented psyche of the post-digital age through CGI, animation, and immersive media. I operationalize its mandate to transform ethics into aesthetics, using technology not only as a tool, but as a lens to expose latent tensions between memory, identity, and constructed realities.

Central to my practice is the EWP Project (2019-2023), CG animation that stages a traveler's return to a morphed hometown. Through non-linear narrative and abstract symbolism, it scrutinizes the instability of "truth", personal history collapses into digital artifacts.

My broader oeuvre, branded eli_lab, extends this investigation into digital melancholy and post-human nostalgia. Works like The Last Day of Kasane Teto repurpose Idol iconography to unpack digital trauma, while Triple Baka transforms autobiographical experience through surrealist animation. My projects induce viewer complicity in constructed fantasies: "darkness" functions not as genre but as critical framework to confront technological disembodiment.

Major Works & Projects

EWP Project (2019-2023)
CG Animation Installation

Concept: A mysterious traveler returns to his transformed hometown, navigating surreal landscapes and fragmented memories to reconcile his dual nature. The work explores universal themes of belonging, memory, and identity through abstract visuals and subconscious symbolism.

The Last Day of Kasane Teto (2025)
Digital Animation / Music Video

Concept: An exploration of digital melancholy through Idol iconography, examining the intersection of virtual identity and human emotion.

Technical Achievement: Created using Blender 4.4.3 with custom eli_lab pipeline extension, demonstrating advanced procedural animation techniques and digital development.

Triple Baka (2025)
Biographical Surrealist Animation

Concept: A week-long intensive production transforming personal autobiography through surrealist visual language. Based on real encounters reinterpreted through Vocaloid cultural references (Teto, Miku, Neru), the work examines how contemporary life becomes "a bad ARG where you suddenly become a character from a decade-old meme."

Technical Achievement: Rapid production methodology and autobiographical-to-digital transformation process.

Daly Syndrome (2023-present)
Animated Film - In Development

Focus: Psychosomatics and cognitive recovery through neural network-generated character movements and gestures, exploring the intersection of artificial intelligence and human psychological expression.
