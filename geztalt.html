Geztålt is a collaborative project and proactive alliance founded by two individuals in the Moscow media industry. 

The project focuses on exploring cultural trends and static forms by decomposing and synthesizing them into artistic statements. 

The project aims to understand how random circumstances come together to form a coherent worldview and how individual associations are influenced by various social contexts and everyday practices. 

Geztålt employs practices of introspection to reveal hidden beliefs through artistic structures such as wordplay, sound performances, and media content. The project incorporates theoretical concepts from anthropology, semantics, epistemology, and media theory to create profound conceptual reinterpretations of everyday experiences. By encouraging viewers to search for their own motivations and cognitive distortions, Geztålt emphasizes play, imitation, and simulation in their artistic process. 

The project also throws electroacoustic and musical parties, claiming to have accidentally become pioneers of Russian hyperpop through their sonic experiments.
